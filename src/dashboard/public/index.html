Step 1 of 3: Contact details

Welcome to casey

casey watches your messages on WhatsApp and Discord and helps you answer them. Here is what this screen shows you, in plain words.

What is each row?

Each row on the left is one person who messaged you, and the whole story of what they need. Click a row to open it.

What is the yellow dot?

A yellow dot means this one is waiting for a person. casey will not answer it on its own. Open it, read it, and reply.

The buttons when you open one

  • How urgent - mark how important it is, so you know what to do first.
  • Who answers - choose who replies to the person: casey on its own, casey writes a draft for you to send, or only you (casey just listens).
  • Reply to contact - type a message and send it to the person yourself.
  • Change the stage - move it along by hand, like marking it Done. The person is not told.

How do I answer someone?

Open the row. Scroll to Reply to contact, type your message, and press Send reply. The person gets it on WhatsApp or Discord.

Tip: the Aa button at the top turns on plain-language labels everywhere.

Keyboard shortcuts (for fast triage)

  • j / k - move down / up the list
  • o or Enter - open the highlighted case
  • c - claim the open case as yours
  • e - jump to the reply box
  • / - search   n - new case   Esc - back / close
  • ? - show this help

You can open this help again any time with the ? button at the top.

Quick start - three things

A new shift starts here. These three steps are all you need to begin.

  1. Pick who you are. Use the name box at the top right so every reply and claim is recorded against you.
  2. The "Needs you now" list is your queue. It puts the cases that cannot wait at the top. Work it from the top down.
  3. Claim a case before you reply. Claiming tells the rest of the team you have it, so two people do not answer the same person.

You can see this again from the ? help, under "Show me the quick start again".

Getting the hang of it

A short checklist of the three moves that make a shift fast. Tick each one as you learn it -- this is just for you, kept on this device, and it will not nag you again once you finish or close it.
