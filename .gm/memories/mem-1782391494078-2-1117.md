## Resolved mutable: mut-child-store-sharing

Resolved by architecture choice, not concurrent-open test: the supervisor uses SEQUENTIAL drain-then-respawn -- the old worker completes graceful drain (in-flight turns finished or marked-for-resume) and fully exits, releasing its sqlite handle, BEFORE the parent forks the new worker which then opens the store. This makes concurrent two-process open of app.db structurally unrepresentable (the exact 'make invalid state unrepresentable' invariant), so WAL cross-process locking semantics never matter to correctness. Cost: a sub-second gap during reload where no new inbound is processed -- acceptable because (a) inbound during the gap is still delivered by the channel and recorded on next boot, (b) reliability-resume-on-boot re-drives any turn stranded by the gap. Witness: exec_js-142 established respawn = fresh module graph reopening the same file; freddie/src/db.js:6 _db singleton re-primes on fresh worker. Zero-downtime overlap is explicitly NOT pursued (it would require the unresolved concurrent-open question); sequential is the safe-by-construction path.
