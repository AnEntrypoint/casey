gm-method (witnessed): a CUT-OFF or mid-typed message ('whats the statu' for 'whats the status') is a real user intent the classifier must handle, not noise -- mobile users send partial messages constantly. When a keyword detector matches only the full word, add the common truncations/prefixes of high-value trigger words as their own tokens (statu/staus/stats for status), but anchor the fuzzy form to a tight frame ('whats the <X>') so a partial word cannot flip the primary/default intent. The lesson generalises: every reported NL miss is one sample of a phrasing CLASS (here: truncations of a known trigger) -- fix the class, add a few representatives to the regression matrix, and re-run the whole matrix to confirm the broadened trigger did not steal a neighbouring intent.
