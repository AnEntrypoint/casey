gm-method lesson (witnessed against a real-services store): an aggregate "is anyone covering?" signal that counts a population (e.g. operator replies in a window) must scope that count to the subpopulation the signal is ABOUT (the breaching cases), not the whole table. Counting globally yields a false NEGATIVE on a busy real store -- replies on unrelated, non-breaching items satisfy the "someone replied" test and suppress the alert, so the rising-edge page never fires. The bug is invisible against a fresh fixture (no unrelated traffic) and only appears against the accumulated live store; therefore witness rising-edge alerts against the busy accumulated store, not a clean one. Fix shape: filter the counted set to the cases that bear on the signal before counting (`if (!isRelevant(c)) continue` ahead of the per-item tally). Generalizes to any "did X happen anywhere" guard layered over a heterogeneous collection.
