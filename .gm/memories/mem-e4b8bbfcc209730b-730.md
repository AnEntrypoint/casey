gm-method (witnessed): a conversational/agent system can be mechanically correct (it drives the dialogue, asks valid-looking questions) yet wrong because the QUESTION WORDING encodes a false assumption about WHO the user is. Here the asks assumed the reporter owned/witnessed the subject, but the real user was a relay reporting a third party. The fix is not in control flow but in the prompt/ask-hint wording and their priority order -- reframe every ask to what the actual persona can observe vs relay, and lead with what they can capture NOW. When a user says 'this question makes no sense for X', map X to the real persona and audit every ask hint + system-prompt audience line against it, not just the one offending question.
