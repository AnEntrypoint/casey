gm-method (witnessed): when a user re-reports a defect you believe is fixed and the symptom looks like a broken feature, the prime suspects are NOT the code -- they are (1) a backend/provider captured once at boot that is merely down (a health endpoint re-probing per request tells the live truth while a boot-captured handle lies; recovery is operational, restart the provider, the self-healing resolver re-binds with no app restart), and (2) the live process predating your fix commit (compare process CreationDate to the fix commit time before re-reading code). A third, subtler trap: recall/auto-recall hits captured DURING a degraded window encode the degraded behavior (canned fallback, re-greeting) and resurface later as if current -- prune them with memorize-prune once the surface recovers, or you debug a phantom. Eliminate each by a real witness against the live service before touching source.
